Veneration

Martin Luther King Jr.[327] was canonized[328] by Archbishop Timothy Paul of the Holy Christian Orthodox Church[329] (not in communion with the Eastern Orthodox Church)[330] on September 9, 2016[331] in the Christian Cathedral in Springfield, Massachusetts,[332] his feast day is April 4, the date of his assassination. King is honored[333] with a Lesser Feast on the liturgical calendar of the Episcopal Church in the United States of America[334] on April 4[335] or January 15.[336] The Evangelical Lutheran Church in America commemorates King liturgically on the anniversary of his birth, January 15
